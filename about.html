About Fort Lewis College

Where Possibility Meets Purpose

Colorado's only public liberal arts college. A place where students discover who they are and who they want to become.

Our Mission

Excellence in the Liberal Arts

Fort Lewis College offers accessible, high-quality baccalaureate liberal arts education to a diverse student population. We prepare our students to become successful learners and leaders in their own lives, the Four Corners region, and the world.

Nestled at 7,600 feet in the San Juan Mountains, our campus provides a unique learning environment where classroom education meets real-world application. Students engage in research, service, and experiential learning from their first semester.

Our Story

Complex History

1891

Established as a military post, Fort Lewis was originally designed to serve the region's growing population.

1911

The federal government transferred the land to Colorado with the requirement that it be used for educational purposes. Fort Lewis School was established as a boarding school for Native American students.

1911-1956

During this era, the institution served primarily Native American students as part of the federal boarding school system. This period represents a complex chapter in our history that we acknowledge with both honesty and a commitment to healing.

1956

Relocated to the current Durango mesa campus and renamed Fort Lewis A&M College. The institution transitioned to become a public college serving all students while maintaining its commitment to Native American education through the tuition waiver program.

Our Heritage

Honoring Our Past, Shaping Our Future

Acknowledging Our History

Fort Lewis College acknowledges its origins as a federal Indian boarding school. We recognize the harm caused by boarding school policies that sought to assimilate Indigenous peoples by separating children from their families, cultures, and languages. This history is part of who we are, and we are committed to truth-telling, healing, and honoring the resilience of Native communities.

The Native American Tuition Waiver

When the federal government transferred the Fort Lewis land to Colorado in 1911, the deed required that Native American students receive free tuition. Today, Fort Lewis College is one of only two institutions in the nation that still honors this commitment. Qualified Native American students who are enrolled members or the children of enrolled members of federally recognized tribes attend tuition-free.

A Native American-Serving Institution

Today, 27% of FLC students are Native American, representing 113 tribal nations. The college is federally designated as a Native American-Serving Non-Tribal Institution. We are committed to supporting Indigenous students' success while celebrating and preserving Native cultures, languages, and traditions. Our Native American Center serves as a home away from home for Indigenous students.
